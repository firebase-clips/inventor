💡 YOUR ORIGINAL IDEA The connecting channel acts as a distributed pressure spine — if any section loses vacuum, neighboring sections feed pressure back in, keeping the whole column stable. This is what makes your multi-section idea genuinely clever.
⚠️ CRITICAL SEAL POINT Every junction between section and connecting channel tap MUST be sealed with silicone. This is the #1 failure point. A single air gap collapses the vacuum in that section.

💡 MT3608 BOOST CONVERTER Your generator may only produce 0.5V–1.5V. The MT3608 is a cheap (~₹30/$0.40) boost module that steps this up to a stable 5V — enough to drive Motor 2 and the fan reliably. Get one from any electronics shop.
06 STEP-BY-STEP ASSEMBLY 🔧
01

BUILD THE TANKS

Top tank: 15×15×8cm plastic container, mount at 80cm height on a wooden frame. Bottom tank: 20×15×10cm at ground level. Drill 1cm holes for siphon pipe connections and seal with silicone.

02

BUILD VACUUM COLUMN — 5 SECTIONS

Cut five 12cm lengths of 3cm PVC pipe. Connect with 2cm gap couplings. At each junction, insert a rubber flap check valve (cut from rubber glove, shaped into a flap). Seal every joint with silicone sealant. Let dry 24 hours before testing.

03

INSTALL CONNECTING CHANNEL

Run 0.8cm PVC tube alongside main column for full 70cm. Drill tiny tap holes at midpoint of each section. Insert small rubber grommet fittings. Your connecting channel idea — this is the pressure equalizer spine. Seal all 5 tap points.

04

INSTALL 4 SIPHON PIPES

Cut four 1cm PVC pipes long enough to peak at 60cm (never exceed 76cm). Arch them from bottom tank up and over, connecting into the top of the vacuum column. Prime each by filling with water completely before connecting — no air gaps.

05

BUILD MANIFOLD + VENTURI

Use three Y-junction PVC fittings to combine 4 siphon outputs into one 3cm pipe. Connect to the glass Venturi tube using rubber grommets at each end. The glass throat section narrows to 1cm over 2cm length with 21° convergence and 7° divergence.

06

BUILD PELTON WHEEL

Glue 12 bottle caps evenly around a 8cm circular PVC disc at 30° spacing. Push a metal rod through the center as shaft. Mount in a small housing so the Venturi nozzle exit is exactly 0.5cm from the cups. The shaft extends to connect to your DC motor.

07

CONNECT GENERATOR + CIRCUIT

Shaft from Pelton wheel → DC motor (now acting as generator). Wire output: + through 1N4007 diode → 1000µF capacitor → MT3608 boost converter → Motor 2. Add solar panel in parallel before the capacitor. Ground all components to common ground rail using 22 AWG wire.

08

INSTALL RETURN PUMP

Small 3V mini submersible pump in bottom tank. Pipe output back up to top tank. Power from the MT3608 output line (solar assists this significantly). This closes the water loop — water flows down through siphons, through turbine, back up to top tank.

09

PRIME AND TEST

Fill both tanks. Manually fill all siphon pipes (use a syringe or blow through them — no air bubbles). Start with just the bottom → top pump running. Once water level in top tank is stable, release siphons one by one. Check for leaks at every sealed joint. Adjust Venturi nozzle distance to Pelton wheel.

10

OPTIMIZE AND CONNECT LOAD

Once spinning, connect Motor 2 → Fan. Add an LED in parallel to visually confirm power. Fine tune the MT3608 output voltage to 3–5V using the small trim pot on the module. Place solar panel in sunlight for maximum sustained runtime.

📐 PRESSURE SCALING WITH MORE SIPHONS ΔP = ½ρ(V₂² − V₁²) — since velocity is squared, doubling siphons gives 4× pressure at the turbine, not 2×. Going from 1 to 4 siphons gives approximately 16× the turbine pressure. This is the mathematical power of your multi-siphon idea.
